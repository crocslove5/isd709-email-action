Dear ISD 709 School Board Members, I am writing to bring attention to an issue that prevents equal access to education for students in our community. Families moving from one home to another within the Duluth school district must complete burdensome paperwork to update their address, which means their children cannot receive school transportation immediately after a move. As a result, students who cannot get to school fall behind. Duluth's housing cost crisis forces many families to move frequently, disrupting their lives and stability. The burden is even greater for students with disabilities and their families. ISD 709 has been asked to address this issue and has been provided with simple solutions. While the school administration has made some attempts, those efforts have not helped, and in some cases have added additional requirements, making things harder for families. The administration and board continue to find reasons to avoid making the changes needed to support families in transition. I urge you to change ISD 709 policy so that students moving from one address to another within Duluth have immediate access to transportation and school. Sincerely, Your Name
